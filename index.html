Individual Values

     In addition to the Base Stats, each individual Pokémon also has Individual Values, or IVs, which make it unique. The four IVs (one for Attack, Defence, Speed, and Special) are random integers ranging from 0 to 15 (i.e. 0000 - 1111). From these, a fifth IV for HP is calculated, and finally, the actual stats are calculated using complicated formulae. We will see this later. For now, click the button to get random IVs for your Pokémon. You may click as many times as you like, but your chance of a perfect 15 for all four IVs is 0.00001525878%. Good luck!

Calculating the HP IV

     As stated before, the Individual Value for HP is calculated from the other four random IVs, and it is done so in a rather interesting way. To your left you see the four IVs which were generated in the last section. They are represented in their usual decimal form as well as in binary. The developers of Pokémon chose to use the least significant bits (i.e. the rightmost digits) of these binary numbers to make the Individual Value for a Pokémon's HP. They are simply arranged in this order to create a new binary number. Click 'Calculate' to see how it works.
